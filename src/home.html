Welcome to the Estimation Calculator

This toolkit is designed to simplify your estimation process for a wide range of construction and project tasks. To get started, simply select a calculator from the list. Each calculator is tailored to a specific trade or task. If you're unsure which calculator to use, browse the equations below or read the instructions included with each tool.

How to Use

  1. Select the appropriate calculator for your estimation task.
  2. Enter your measurements and variables in the provided fields.
  3. Review the calculated results, which are based on industry-standard formulas.
  4. Adjust variables as needed to refine your estimate.

As you explore, feel free to suggest new calculators or equations for inclusion. This toolkit is a living resource and will grow over time!

Common Estimation Equations

Area of a Rectangle

Area = Length × Width

Useful for floor, wall, or roof area calculations.

Volume of a Cuboid (ft³ or yd³)

Volume = Length × Width × Height

Convert cubic feet to cubic yards by dividing the result by 27.

Circular Area

Area = π × Radius²

Use for round slabs, piers, or tanks.

Right Triangle (Pythagorean Theorem)

c² = a² + b²

Ideal for slopes, diagonals, or rafter length calculations.

Material Waste Factor

Total Required = Base Quantity × (1 + Waste Percentage)

Add a typical waste factor (e.g., 10%) for cutting and fitting materials.

Man-Hours Calculation

Man-Hours = Total Work Units ÷ Productivity Rate

For example, 2,000 sq ft at 50 sq ft/hour → 2,000 ÷ 50 = 40 man-hours.
